Ballydehob Repair Café

Hello! We're the — a volunteer-run community repair space where broken things get a second chance, and people get to learn a skill or two along the way.

We meet in the Ballydehob Bank House on the third Friday of each month, from 3pm to 5:30pm. You don't need to book. You don't need any experience. You just need to bring whatever's stopped working and a willingness to have a go at fixing it with us.

Our volunteers come from all sorts of backgrounds — electronics engineers, hobbyist tinkerers, retired technicians, and curious beginners. We won't be able to fix everything — but we'll always have a proper look and share what we find.

Next Repair Café day is Friday March 20th, when we will be celebrating the Café's third birthday! See you then!

What is a repair cafe?

Repair cafés are community events where people get together to fix broken things for free, instead of throwing them away. Repairing and re-using is satisfying, reduces waste and landfill, extends the life of things, and saves people spending money on repairs or buying new.

At a Repair Café, 'Fixer' volunteers work alongside people who have brought in broken items, sharing and spreading repair skills. People bring broken household objects and appliances, furniture and toys, clothes, textiles, jewellery and much more. There is also tea, cake & chat!

There is an international movement of Repair Cafés who offer guidance for those starting out.

The Ballydehob Repair Café has become a regular event — now, in March 2026, we are about to celebrate our third birthday!

What Makes Us Tick?

  • Fixers — A team of local volunteers with a range of skills, expertise, and tools.
  • Organisers — Roles include publicity, admin, organising the café, meet and greet, tea and cakes, setting up and clearing up.
  • Venue Room — A space with tables, chairs, and facilities for tea. We've been lucky to use the Ballydehob Bank House, but due to expanding interest, we're hoping to find a larger space in the future.
  • People & Visitors — People who come to the café and bring their broken items. That's you!

History

The first Repair Café took place in the Bank House, Ballydehob on 8 March 2024. Thirty-one visitors dropped in, representing a mix of ages and mostly local residents with a few travellers from Schull, Bantry, and Roscarberry.

Present that day were Dom (appliances/bikes/general), Jens (woodwork/general), Ger (sewing/mending) and Fiona (general). Several helpers signed up for future sessions including Nick (electrical), Josephine (sewing/mending), Walter (general) and members of Cycle Sense.

Items worked on included lamps, a school bag, ukulele, electric heater, garden fork, gramophones, and clothing; advice and lessons were given for sewing, knitting and coffee machines. The event showed the value of hands‑on repair and the friendly community spirit around it.

The café now meets on the third Friday of every month, with the next session on Friday 19 April at the Bank House. Volunteers are welcome – fixers, greeters, tea‑makers, setup/clear‑up helpers and more. Contact gercooney@mundusloci.org to join the team.
